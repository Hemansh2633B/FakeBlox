Overworld Landing Page

Plains

Plains – A relatively flat area that may have some trees. You can find villages and farm animals like horses.
Ice plains – Also called snowy tundra. It's a snow and ice-covered area with very few trees that is mostly flat but has some hills.
Ice spike plains – A variant of the ice plains that have large icy spikes and glaciers that reach into the sky.
Sunflower plains – The only biome where sunflowers spawn.
Snowy plains – A snow-covered area that can have rabbits, polar bears, and strays. Igloos may generate.
Mushroom field – An island made of mycelium where big mushrooms grow frequently. The only area where you can find mooshrooms.
Savanna – A flat, dry area that has acacia trees and lots of tall grass.

Woodlands

Forest – Areas filled with oak and birch trees. Mushrooms, flowers, and wolves can also appear.
Birch forest – Variant of the forest where only birch trees appear. Wolves do not spawn here.
Dark forest – Forest variant filled with dark oak trees. Trees are packed so tightly hostile mobs can spawn in daytime. Woodland mansions appear here.
Flower forest – Fewer trees appear, replaced by lots of flowers. Rabbits replace wolves in these areas.
Old growth birch forest – A birch forest with much taller trees.
Taiga – A forest of spruce trees with ferns, berry bushes, foxes, and rabbits.
Old growth spruce taiga – A taiga variant where giant spruce trees are covered in leaf blocks.
Old growth pine taiga – A taiga variant with taller/wider spruce trees plus mossy cobblestone and podzol.
Snowy taiga – A snowy taiga variant where igloos can generate.
Jungle – Dense biome of tall jungle trees with vines, ocelots, parrots, pandas, cocoa, and melons.
Bamboo jungle – A jungle covered in trees and bamboo thickets. Pandas spawn more often.
Sparse jungle – A less dense jungle with more spacing.
Grove – Spruce forest at mountain bottoms near forest biomes, with snow and powder snow.
Cherry Grove – Area with pink cherry trees and falling petals.
Pale Garden – A dark area with the creaking mob, pale oak wood, and pale moss items.

Caves

Deep dark – Underground sculk biome containing ancient cities and the warden.
Dripstone caves – Exclusive place to find dripstone blocks.
Lush caves – Caverns with moss, vines, glow berries, and axolotls.

Mountains

Jagged peaks – Highest mountain peaks with snow, stone, coal, iron, and emerald.
Frozen peaks – Peaks filled with ice, snow, and packed ice.
Stony peaks – Warmer peaks without snow or goats.
Snowy slopes – Snow-covered mountain slopes with goats and rabbits.
Windswept hills – Steep cliffs, valleys, and floating islands with llamas and emeralds.
Windswept forest – Windswept hills beside forests, with grassy floor and trees.
Windswept gravelly hills – Mostly gravel-filled variant of windswept hills.
Meadow – Flowery grassland near mountains; trees here always have bee nests.
Stony shores – Stone coastlines where mountains meet oceans; buried treasure may appear.
Savanna Plateau – Higher-altitude savanna in mountainous zones.
Windswept savanna – Mountainous savanna with steep, high cliffs.

Swamps

Swamp – Dark wet biome with shallow water, vines, frogs, blue orchids, and swamp huts.
Mangrove swamp – Vegetation-rich swamp with mangrove trees and mud blocks.

Sandy Areas

Badlands – Red-sand desert with abundant terracotta and stained terracotta.
Wooded badlands – Badlands variant with coarse dirt and oak trees.
Eroded badlands – Tall terracotta spires shoot into the sky.
Beach – Sandy shoreline where turtles spawn and buried treasure may appear.
Snowy beach – Frozen beach variant where turtles do not spawn.
Desert – Sandy area with cacti, fossils, husks, and structures like villages/pyramids.

Water Areas

River – Water channels through land containing salmon, squid, and drowned.
Frozen river – Icy river with salmon and drowned underwater; rabbits and polar bears above.
Ocean – Basic ocean with seagrass, kelp, and fish.
Cold ocean – Deep-blue ocean with less wildlife and large kelp groups.
Deep ocean – Deeper ocean with ravines and magma blocks; includes frozen/cold/lukewarm variants.
Frozen ocean – Icy ocean with salmon, squid, monuments, and occasional polar bears.
Lukewarm ocean – No coral reefs, but more seagrass, cod, salmon, and kelp.
Warm ocean – Vibrant ocean with coral reefs, dolphins, pufferfish, and tropical fish.
